Here is some advice on how to train if you're just starting:

Warm Up

Not warming up is a mistake many beginners make. Before any session ensure that you warm up thoroughly and mobilise the areas that you will be using during the workout. Furthermore, failure to warm up properly may increase the risk of injury.

Consistency is Key

Results come through tearing the muscle fibers regularly and consistently. Make sure you follow a daily routine and plan out the times that work best for you (1-2 hour sessions max).

Never Give Up

It can be discouraging to have setbacks, no matter how minor- remember all of us are human, and bad days come and go. Focus on the end goal, re-evaluate, and go from there.

Diet

Diet is extremely important, and you have to make sure that you are fueling your body correctly. Try to consume at least 400 calories above your maintenaince calories to start, slowly easing into a calorie surplus.

Advice for intermediate trainers:

When I was a beginner:

When I was a beginner, I did it all wrong. I would train very inconsistently (on and off), and I would never diet correctly. This is pointless especially when trying to achieve results due to the fact that you need to fuel your body with the sufficient macronutrients and calories when trying to repair damaged tissue.

My general advice for any beginner, is to skip this phase. Regardless of if you are eating the wrong macronutrients, when attempting to gain weight, keep the calories and carbohydrates relatively high, especially if you are doing strength training (high weight, low reps).
