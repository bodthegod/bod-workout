Here is some advice on how to train if you're just starting:

Warm Up

Not warming up is a mistake many beginners make. Before any session ensure that you warm up thoroughly and mobilise the areas that you will be using during the workout. Furthermore, failure to warm up properly may increase the risk of injury.

Consistency is Key

Results come through tearing the muscle fibers regularly and consistently. Make sure you follow a routine and plan out the times that work best for you (1-2 hour sessions max).

Never Give Up

It can be discouraging to have setbacks, no matter how minor- remember all of us are human, and bad days come and go. Focus on the end goal, re-evaluate, and go from there.

Diet

Diet is extremely important, and you have to make sure that you are fueling your body correctly. Try to consume at least 400 calories above your maintenaince calories to start, slowly easing into a calorie surplus.

My Love for Fitness:

My fitness journey initially started because I never had the self confidence to speak to people, and I was extremely introverted when I was in secondary school, and throughout the years after.

The second I set foot in the gym, instantly I knew it would be an extreme challenge for me, and because of that, my dedication slowly started to build. Many years down the line after I had went to the gym as consistently as I possibly could, something clicked in my head and at that moment, I decided to dedicate as much time as I had in my free time to bettering myself physically, and mentally.
