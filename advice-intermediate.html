Here are the techniques I used when I was an intermediate trainer:

Strength Training

Once you have learned all the basics, strength training is the first thing I recommend learning more about, this is because strength is the foundations of a stronger body, and if you want to get more experienced, this stage is key in establishing that.

Form > Weight

Form will always be one of the things you always need to attempt to keep on point, this means if you can't keep the muscles under tension for long enough to feel it, drop the weight and focus on tearing the muscle, instead of lifting as heavy as you can.

Range of Motion

When lifting, always focus on the range of motion. This is extremely vital when training as the longer the muscles are under tension (time under tension) the more microscopic tears there will be within your muscles. This then allows for them to grow back bigger and stronger, provided you are eating the correct protein intake.

Rest

Rest can be just as important as your diet, and this is because when resting, this is where our body repairs the most. Make sure to sleep for at least 7 hours a night, to make sure your muscles repair as quick as possible. This is because protein synthesis is more active when sleeping, proving to be vital to any gym-goer.

When I was an Intermediate Trainer

When I was an intermediate trainer, I made mistakes and even now I make mistakes when I'm in the gym, but that's all a part of the journey and learning new excersises and tips and tricks can mix things up and make the gym fun again.

The most important thing I feel you should focus on as an intermediate trainer, is diet. Regardless of how hard you train- figuring out your diet can be vital in order to increase strength, and in order to get your desired physique, keeping a clean diet with a high protein intake is vital to achieve these goals. My best advice is to reinvent the way you eat, and try to cut out the processed foods.
